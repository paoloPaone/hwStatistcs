Sampling Means Distribution

The graph below illustrates the distribution of sample means obtained from m = 1000 samples of size n = 20, derived from the theoretical discrete parent distribution [0, 1, 2] with probabilities [0.2, 0.3, 0.5]. The X-axis represents the frequencies of the sample means, and the Y-axis shows the intervals (bins) of the sample means. The "Mean of Means" is 1.30, closely aligning with the theoretical mean of the parent distribution. The variance of the sample means, 0.03, is smaller than that of the parent distribution, consistent with the formula Variance of Means = Parent Distribution Variance / n . This reflects the Central Limit Theorem, which predicts that the sample means will approximate a normal distribution as n and m grow.
